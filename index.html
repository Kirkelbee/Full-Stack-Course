Studying Full Stack Development

21 April 2025

In January 2024 I had never really paid any attention to how websites worked - I was a typical user of web browsers, email, a consumer of news, and an occasional online shopper. I had basically heard of HTML files at some point in ancient history and that was my sum total of technical knowledge.

All this changed when I decided to pursue a new career after nearly 25 years in the construction sector. I initially started with Udemy courses for Python, PostgreSQL, and C#. My existing mathematics and engineering skills were obviously helpful, but I didn't really understand how things fitted together and I found myself growing frustrated.

Then in December 2025 I discovered Angela Yu's "Full Stack Development" course on Udemy and I finally started feeling like I was really making progress. It's been an exciting and rewarding 4 months. As at Easter 2025 I am nearly finished with the front-end modules and before starting the back-end work I thought it might be good revision to repeat some of the assignments and upload them to my Github space.

I'm posting everything in it's rawest, ugliest state to remind me how far I've come.

I'll also link my keystone projects, and some of Angela's useful or cool websites.

One day I'll spend some time making this page look good, or I'll incorporate it into a real portfolio, but for now it is purely serving as a revision tool for me.

Mark Kirkness
